Conference Call History

A Conference call, or multi party phone connections has roots in what was known as a party line. In this setup a phone call was physically connected to multiple handsets by wires where more than one handset could be picked up at a time. In some rural areas houshold phones are still setup in this fashion where RJ11 analog is terminated at multiple handsets in a single or multiple dwelling including barns and outhouses. As time went on many households moved away from a 'lan' or LAND line and to save money have moved primarily to mobiles, handys, or as are referred to in the U.S. 'Cell Phones'. Now we have a problem, there are no phsyical connections to pickup up multiple handsets as the wires are missing! This is where Conference Calls come in. Today we Use VoIP (Voice Over IP = Internet Protocol) to make these connections but just a few years ago a PBX (Private Branch Exchange) would have been used. Ginormous companies like Cisco and Polycom showed up to offer hardware for the small business owner to host their own phone systems and conducty their own conference calls, but these are now irrelevent and the decline in their stock price and user base reflects this shift. Internet VoIP based conference calls allow anyone, anywhere throughout the world to conduct multi party conversations where with only one loss of function, the NSA and other world TLA (Three Letter Agencies) are able to monitor and influence the direction of business. These tools are so powerful, even the global conglomerant Microsoft saw the power in this and purchased Skype for 8.5 BILLION dollars in 2011.
