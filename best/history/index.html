sMoreMail History

SmoreMail began in 2013 with a small team of independant researchers looking to troubleshoot private email systems and train local spam engines. Most researchers and IT professionals use a responsive technique to catelog and signature unwanted email only after it is been seen live on the network. As techniques improve, so did methods to get ahead of this issue in a corporate environment so the basic concept of sMoreMail was conceived. There is a similar method to sMoreMail but those developers have since parted ways and the engine here is published for the first time starting in 2019 for others to use.

Commercial mail engines continue to improve, and even personal servers are now accessible with a acceptable spam engines, but for fully clean operations it remains a challenge to stay ahead of the spam and unwanted email techniques. As a private user, one of the best things you an do is use the 'unsubscribe' links in unwanted emails or use your client to automatically click these on your behalf, but some malicious senders will not respect these links.

The can spam act forbids legitimate sources from continuously emailing and not respecting these links, but rouge bot nets pay no mind to this suggestion. For this reason, sMoreMail is an invaluable resource to the IT researcher and professional in identifying this rouge content early, before it is used by the user base and train the local rules to identify, quarantine and remove email before it hits users inboxes that are often not knowledgable enough to know not to click random links in and download random .EXE in email.

In 2016, several networks were crippled with ransomware that took small local businesses offline when unkowning users clicked links in their emails. In these attacks, the victims computer was used to encrypt network files silently - but wait was programmed in a way that it took over a month to detect. In many small businesses, the IT group is small and not in a position to maintain 100% incremental and full tape backups and the backup tapes were found to also be encrypted where the IT folks did not know the keys.

This ransomware as it was later named - would hold the very network of the small business hostage until suffiicent bitcoins were transfered. Don't let this happen to you! Run sMoreMail early, train your systems, train your users - and protect your users from unwanted email!
